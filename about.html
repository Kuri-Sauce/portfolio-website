My Story

Hello there!

My name is Kari. I live in the San Francisco Bay Area, in a quaint town called Benicia. In a successful second attempt, I drove across the country to the Bay Area eight years ago with just my two fur kids and whatever else I could fit into my Tiburon. My first attempt ended with my newly bought 26-year-old RV breaking down after driving it 276 miles into the 1730-mile-trip. It’s been quite the journey ever since.

In my free time, I connect with loved ones, play games, walk my pup, and relish my me-time. The self-proclaimed definition of “me-time” is a period in which a person provides themselves self-care and rejects any agenda but their own. I am also an improviser and a soccer coach for a San Francisco–based nonprofit organization that trains young women in leadership and life skills through soccer called Girls Leading Girls.

My professional background includes three years in administration and 13 years in customer service and hospitality as a server, bartender, and manager in restaurants. My journey to becoming a UX/UI Designer started when I was working as the administrator for an improv company in SF. I began using AppSheet to build a clocking system for the employees and discovered I enjoyed making these web applications. I discovered that my love for problem-solving and creating a stress-free experience can be expressed through user experience and user interface designing.
