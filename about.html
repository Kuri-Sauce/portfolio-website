About

My Story

Hello there!

My name is Kari. I live in the San Francisco Bay Area in a quaint town called Benicia. In a successful second attempt, I drove across the country to the Bay Area 8 years ago with just my two fur kids and whatever else I could fit into my Tiburon. My first attempt ended with my newly bought 26-year-old RV breaking down after driving it 276 miles into the 1730 mile trip. It’s been quite the journey ever since.

In my free time, I connect with loved ones, play games, walk my pup, and relish me-time. The self-proclaimed definition of “me-time” is a period of time in which a person provides themselves self-care and rejects any agenda but their own. It comes highly recommended. I am also an improviser, as well as, a soccer coach for a San Francisco–based nonprofit organization that trains young women in leadership and life skills through soccer called Girls Leading Girls.

My professional background includes 3 years in administration and 13 years in customer service and hospitality as a server, bartender, and manager in restaurants. My journey to becoming a UX/UI Designer started when I was working as the administrator for an improv company in SF. I began using AppSheet to build a clocking system for the employees and discovered I really enjoyed making these web applications. I discovered that my love for problem-solving and creating a stress-free experience can be expressed through user experience and user interface designing.
