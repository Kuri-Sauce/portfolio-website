My Story

Hello there!

My name is Kari. I live in the San Francisco Bay Area, in a quaint town called Benicia. I am originally from the east coast, and it took me a couple of tries before I finally arrived here. My first attempt ended with my newly bought 26-year-old RV breaking down after driving it 276 miles into the 1730-mile trip. In a successful second attempt, I drove across the country to the Bay Area eight years ago with my two fur kids and whatever else I could fit into my Tiburon. It’s been quite the journey ever since.

I'm a UX/UI Designer that values connection. As the wise Brené Brown said, "I define connection as the energy that exists between people when they feel seen, heard, and valued; when they give and receive without judgment; and when they derive sustenance and strength from the relationship." Whew! I got goosebumps from simply typing that quote. Taking the time to connect with users, coworkers, and clients allows me to empathize with their experiences and open my perspective to new solutions. And this is also a huge reason I love to perform improv! As an improviser, I get to explore the possibilities outside the box by saying "yes" to ideas both on stage and as a designer.

In my free time, I connect with loved ones, play games, walk my pup, and relish my me-time. The self-proclaimed definition of “me-time” is a period in which a person provides themselves self-care and rejects any agenda but their own. And a few days a week, I coach soccer for a nonprofit organization in San Francisco that trains girls in leadership and life skills through soccer called Girls Leading Girls.
