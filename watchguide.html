WatchGuide

WatchGuide will help all users gather all of the shows and movies they want to watch from various service providers into one app and allow the user to create their own streaming schedule.

WatchGuide was inspired by my desire to browse and watch all the streaming content I have available in one spot rather than hopping from one service provider to the next or being overwhelmed with content not readily available to me. My goal for this project was to create a simple design with a satisfying solution.

Mid Fidelity Wireframes

Imagery & UI

WatchGuide uses minimal color and a simple layout giving a familiar appearance to provide an ease of transition from other service provider interfaces while setting the perfect mood for streaming.

Deciding on the color was challenging given the various streaming apps' signature color palettes many people already recognize. My solution was to discover what helps set the mood for streaming by exploring the environment using all my senses to see how it affected my mood. And find out what got me the most excited to stream.

Comfortable, familiar, and immersive experience.

The WatchGuide logo is meant to evoke the feelings of nostalgia and excitement over the anticipation of what is next to watch in the same way the TV Guide did for us kids who grew up in the 90s.

The subtle gold light leaking in from the bottom emulates the light from a movie projector in the theater or a crackling fire at home creating a warm and soothing environment.

High Fidelity Wireframes

Prototyping & User Feedback

Figma

Using Figma to prototype was very useful for it’s real time collaboration allowing for an efficient feedback process. I could quickly organize the information for review, find patterns, and begin brainstorming.

60% of users reported being confused by the Add icon’s function in the bottom navigation bar. So, I changed the icon and name to better communicate its function and improve the user’s experience.

60% of users were not confident they had completed their task to add content to their watch list. A checkmark was added onto the image card to signify their task was completed.
